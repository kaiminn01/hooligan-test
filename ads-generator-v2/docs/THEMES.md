# Pain Theme System

## What is a theme?

A theme is a cluster of related complaints that real people posted about on Reddit, YouTube, Hacker News, or Product Hunt. The AI groups similar complaints together and gives the cluster a name.

Example: If 12 people said things like "my OpenClaw keeps going offline", "it crashes at night", "have to restart it every morning" — these all get grouped into one theme called "Uptime & Reliability".

---

## Why group into themes instead of individual complaints?

Because one Google Ad Group should target one pain point. If you mix pain points in one ad, the message is diluted and nobody clicks.

Each theme = one Ad Group in Google Ads = one set of headlines targeting that specific pain.

---

## Pain level explained

Every theme gets a pain level based on the language people used:

### 🔴 Critical pain
- Language: "I'm so frustrated", "this is unusable", "I've tried everything", "switching to X", "lost X hours"
- Behaviour: People actively seeking alternatives, complaining publicly, venting
- Ad opportunity: Highest. These people are ready to buy a solution RIGHT NOW.

### 🟠 Moderate pain
- Language: "it's annoying", "wish this worked better", "it could be improved"
- Behaviour: Tolerating the problem but open to a better solution
- Ad opportunity: Medium. Needs stronger offer to motivate action.

### 🟡 Mild pain
- Language: "would be nice if", "minor issue", "not a big deal but..."
- Behaviour: Aware of the problem, not urgently seeking a fix
- Ad opportunity: Low. Use for retargeting only, not cold traffic.

---

## Confidence score

Each theme also gets a confidence rating:

- **High confidence** — multiple independent sources express the same pain in similar words
- **Medium confidence** — fewer posts but consistent pattern
- **Low confidence** — only 1-2 posts, could be an outlier

Low confidence themes should be deprioritised or skipped unless they match your intuition about the market.

---

## How to use themes in Google Ads

1. Each approved theme becomes one **Ad Group**
2. All headlines for that theme go into one **Responsive Search Ad**
3. The theme name becomes the **Ad Group name** in your CSV
4. Target keywords for that Ad Group should match the theme's pain (e.g. "openclaw keeps crashing", "openclaw downtime fix")

---

## Theme grouping logic

The AI looks for:
1. Shared emotional tone (frustration, confusion, urgency)
2. Shared root cause (same underlying problem)
3. Shared desired outcome (what the person wants instead)

Two complaints about different symptoms of the same root problem get grouped together. Two complaints about different root problems stay separate — even if they sound similar on the surface.
