# Ad Strength Scoring System

Every headline and description is scored out of 100. Here's exactly how points are awarded.

---

## Headlines (max 30 characters)

| Criteria | Points | What it checks |
|---|---|---|
| Character limit | 30 pts | Must be 30 chars or under. Over limit = 0 total score. |
| Keyword present | 25 pts | Does the headline contain one of your target keywords? Google bolds matching words, increasing CTR. |
| Pain signal | 20 pts | Does it reference a specific frustration? Words like "crash", "down", "fix", "tired", "broken", "stuck" score here. |
| Outcome/number | 15 pts | Specific outcomes convert better. Numbers (60s, $14, 24/7), time words (instant, minutes), or definitive words (never, always, guaranteed). |
| Not generic | 10 pts | Penalises vague words like "best", "great", "solution", "platform", "easy". These get ignored. |

**Score bands:**
- 80-100 = Ready to use ✅
- 60-79 = Review before using 🟠
- Below 60 = Needs rewrite ❌

---

## Descriptions (max 90 characters)

| Criteria | Points | What it checks |
|---|---|---|
| Character limit | 30 pts | Must be 90 chars or under. Over limit = 0 total score. |
| Clear benefit | 35 pts | Does it show what the user gains? "No DevOps", "always online", "deploy in 60s", "$14/mo" all score here. |
| Call to action | 20 pts | Soft CTA words: get, start, try, deploy, move, switch, join, see, discover. |
| Specific enough | 15 pts | Must be more than 8 words. Short descriptions lack conviction. |

---

## Variation strategy

Each theme gets 10-15 headline variations. They are NOT the same thing reworded — each targets a different angle:

| Variation type | Example | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Problem-led | "OpenClaw Down Again?" | Speaks to active frustration |
| Solution-led | "Always-On OpenClaw Host" | Speaks to desired outcome |
| Offer-led | "OpenClaw for $14/mo" | Price-motivated searchers |
| Fear-led | "Stop Losing Work to Crashes" | Loss aversion |
| Question | "Tired of Restarting OpenClaw?" | Conversational, high CTR |
| Keyword-mirror | "OpenClaw Managed Hosting" | Exact match to search query |
| Competitor-counter | "Better Than MyClaw" | Conquest traffic |

You run all variations in one Responsive Search Ad. Google automatically tests combinations and surfaces the best performers. More variations = more data = faster learning.

---

## Pain level ratings

| Level | What it means | Bid strategy |
|---|---|---|
| 🔴 Critical | People losing money, switching products, very vocal | Highest bid, most budget |
| 🟠 Moderate | Regular annoyance, workarounds exist | Medium bid |
| 🟡 Mild | Nice to fix, not urgent | Low bid, test only |

Critical pain themes should get the most ad budget because searchers are actively motivated to solve the problem right now.

---

## Signal count explained

"18 signals" means 18 out of the total scraped posts/comments expressed this pain point.

Sources counted:
- Reddit posts where the title or body mentions this pain
- YouTube comments expressing this frustration

Higher signals = more people have this problem = higher potential audience size in Google Ads.

**Important**: Signal count is relative to your scrape, not absolute. 18/77 scraped = 23% of your audience has this pain. That's significant.
